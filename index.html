Course Description & Core Principles:

This course focuses on digital remediation of academic work, transforming scholarly writing for web publication through design thinking and digital liberal arts practice. Students will master CRAP principles (Contrast, Repetition, Alignment, Proximity) while developing proficiency in HTML5, CSS3, and responsive design. Through a progressive lab structure, students create a professional digital publication that makes their academic work accessible to broader audiences. The course emphasizes public scholarship, accessibility, and professional workflow using industry-standard tools. No prior coding experience is required. By the end of the course, students will have transformed their scholarly work into an engaging digital presence while understanding how design choices shape scholarly communication.

Core Principles

  • Digital remediation focus - transforming academic work for web publication and broader accessibility
  • CRAP design principles - mastering contrast, repetition, alignment, and proximity as foundational framework
  • Progressive lab structure - four interconnected labs building toward successful academic content remediation
  • Public scholarship emphasis - making scholarly work accessible through thoughtful digital design
  • Professional workflow integration - GitHub, version control, and collaborative development practices
